A Chair from Copenhagen

Learning and Exploring

During the summer of 2022 I decided to study abroad in Copenhagen, Denmark. For two months I studied furniture design, visited designers, museums, and creators with the final assignment of producing a full sized chair.

As we learned and designed within our studios, we created initial designs, prototypes, and scale drawings as we prepared to create our final chair.

Taking inspiration from the Spanish Chair by Børge Mogensen and the environment that surrounded me during the trip, my goal was to create a lounge chair that was chunky, comfy, and simply.

Initial Designs

To begin the process, started with various potential designs. At first I focused on the structure, creating various ways the chair would sit, where the joints would go, and how the armrests would be styled.

From there I figured out how I would want to connect the two sides, creating options that included all wood, woven fabric, and even cushions.

My intentions for this chair was to focus on the construction and understand how chairs are built. As I created variations I finally found a design that was most realistic and suitable for the work we would be doing in such a short time frame.

Prototypes

After creating initial sketches I moved on to produce a 1:5 scale model and then a 1:1 scale model to understand the dimensions and materials needed before producing the final piece. As I created each model, I discovered issues with my designs each time and altered them in autoCAD to be more functional.

I redesigned the stretchers (which hold the two sides together) and their positions so it created natural tension for the woven fabric, making it a more comfortable experience. As I prototyped I finalized my chair and prepared for final production.

Construction

Spending two weeks in the wood shop, I worked with talented cabinetmakers and designers, who taught us the skills needed to create our chairs. From raw oak wood I slowly produced my lounge chair, learning about the Danish design process along the way.

Once finally crafted, I sanded the chair until smooth and finished it with soap, a mixture Danish cabinet makers use to maintain the natural finish while protecting the wood from outside elements. For the weaving fabric I sourced mauve cotton straps that would be used for the back and seat rest.

Final Piece

In the end, with no time to spare, I was able to fulfill my goal of creating a lounge chair that took inspiration from the Spanish Chair. With a chunky style and large armrests, the chair I created is meant to be comfortable, functional, and simple. I wanted a chair I could read in, work in, and even sit criss cross in.

While I'm happy with the final product there’s a lot of work the chair still needs if I were to reproduce it. Through my time in Copenhagen, I was able to learn about the culture and history of its people and their infamous furniture design.

Along this exciting and tiring journey I learned that furniture production and design takes many iterations until you get it just right. While my chair is nowhere near perfect, I was able to so much from it and I can’t wait to take those skills and keep learning more.
